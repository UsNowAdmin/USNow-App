55 Years of Commitment

U.S. military assistance to Israel began in earnest after the 1973 Yom Kippur War and has never stopped. The 2016 Memorandum of Understanding locked in $3.8 billion per year through 2028 — the largest military aid package the U.S. has ever provided to any country. After October 7, 2023, Congress approved an additional $14.1 billion emergency package on top of the baseline, bringing the post-Oct. 7 total to over $21.7 billion in roughly 18 months.

The $330 billion inflation-adjusted cumulative figure covers all U.S. assistance since Israeli statehood in 1948. Of that, $244 billion is military. No other bilateral relationship in American foreign policy history has this financial profile — sustained, bipartisan, and almost entirely exempt from the conditions attached to other foreign aid.
