How the Mountain Was Built

From 1948 to 1961 the U.S. provided economic aid only — deliberately avoiding military assistance to stay neutral with Arab oil states. France was Israel's arms supplier. The Six Day War in 1967 changed the calculation: France imposed an arms embargo and the U.S. stepped into the vacuum. By 1971 Congress had earmarked Israeli aid for the first time and military assistance jumped tenfold. The 1973 Yom Kippur War produced the first massive spike — an emergency $2.6B airlift.

Camp David in 1979 institutionalized the commitment. Aid became all-grant in 1985 — no repayment ever required. The 2016 MOU locked $3.8B per year through 2028, the largest military aid package the U.S. has ever given any country. Then October 7, 2023 produced the eruption at the summit — over $17B in a single fiscal year, more than any previous decade combined.
