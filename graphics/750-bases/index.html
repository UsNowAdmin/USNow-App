From Orbit, This Is What Empire Looks Like

Every dot is a real U.S. military installation — base, outpost, forward operating site, cooperative security location. The clusters over Japan, Korea, Germany, and the Gulf aren't accidents. They are the physical residue of decisions made in the 1940s, 50s, and after 9/11 that were never reversed. The vast dark spaces — South America, Central Africa, Central Asia — tell you just as much as the bright clusters.

The Soviet Union that justified most of this is gone. The question of whether 750 installations in 80 countries matches the actual threat environment of 2025 is one of the most expensive questions America isn't asking.
