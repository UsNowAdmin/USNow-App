Why the Number Is Always Wrong

The $886B figure that appears in headlines is the Pentagon's discretionary budget request. It does not include the nuclear arsenal (managed by the Department of Energy), veterans' care (Department of Veterans Affairs), the interest accumulating on debt from past wars, or foreign military assistance flowing through the State Department.

When you add those numbers — all of which are direct consequences of military policy — the true annual cost of American defense posture exceeds $2.3 trillion. That is roughly 10 cents of every dollar the U.S. economy produces, every year. The gap between the advertised number and the real one is not an accident. It is a design.
