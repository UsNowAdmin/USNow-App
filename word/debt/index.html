Debt

Investment, trap, or moral failing — depending on who owes.
How We Say It
det
Where It Comes From
Latin debitum — that which is owed. Related to debere — to owe. The silent b was reinserted in the 16th century by scholars wanting to honor the Latin root, overriding centuries of spoken English that had dropped it.
How It's Been Used
Carries moral weight beyond the financial — 'in debt' implies obligation and shame in ways that 'owe money' does not. Debt forgiveness invokes this moral dimension. The framing of national debt as household debt is one of the most consequential and contested metaphors in economics.
