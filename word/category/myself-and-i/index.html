Myself & I

14 entries · alphabetical
— A —
  • Addiction When we feed our body what it wants beyond the point of what it needs — drugs, money, power, or anything.
— B —
  • Body Self, vessel, political battleground, or crime scene.
— C —
  • Consciousness The subjective state of one's awareness and knowing — our personal version of the universe. Indescribable. Cannot be measured, verified, or transferred.
— D —
  • Death Our end, a transition, or the thing that makes everything else matter.
— E —
  • Emotion Things we feel and often act on — fear, anger, pride, etc. May be our own, but also induced, coerced, or manufactured.
— F —
  • Failure When things don't go as planned or expected. Often seen as a negative — but the best teacher we may ever have. (compare with Success)
  • Fear Perhaps the prime mover of all human behavior. (see Emotion)
— H —
  • Health Physical state, commercial industry, or human right.
  • Home Where we're from, where we live, and where we belong — defined by us and by others.
— L —
  • Life Biological and biographical — defined by us and by others.
  • Love Connection and caring for another. Not possessive or conditional. Our driving force and only real salvation. Whether we realize it or not.
— P —
  • Pain Significant physical, psychological, or existential suffering. Always relative. Perhaps the price of too much pleasure, or just being alive. (see also Pleasure or Suffering)
  • Pleasure The opposite, or flip side, of pain and suffering. What we seek, what we're sold, and what we're warned to moderate. (see also Pain or Suffering)
— S —
  • Suffering The sustained experience of pain — physical, psychological, or existential discomfort and discontent. What we try to avoid, what we tend to cause, and probably a purpose or result of life. (see also Pain or Pleasure)
