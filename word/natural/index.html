Natural

From nature, or morally correct. The confusion sells.
How We Say It
nach·ər·əl
Where It Comes From
Latin naturalis — by birth, according to nature. From natura — nature, character, essence. From nasci — to be born. Natural, nation, and native all share the birth root — what is natural is what was there at birth.
How It's Been Used
'Natural' is among the most powerful rhetorical moves in moral argument — if something is natural, it appears beyond human choice or criticism. 'Unnatural' works the same way in reverse. The fallacy of moving from 'is natural' to 'is good' — the naturalistic fallacy — was identified by philosophers but thrives in everyday argument.
