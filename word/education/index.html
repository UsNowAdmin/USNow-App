Education

Learning, indoctrination, credentials, or liberation — depends.
How We Say It
ej·oo·kay·shən
Where It Comes From
Latin educatio — a rearing, training. From educare — to bring up, rear. Related to educere — to lead out. The two roots suggest different models: filling (bringing up) versus drawing out what is already there.
How It's Been Used
The tension between those two etymological models — deposit vs. liberation — runs through every education debate. Paulo Freire named it explicitly in 1968. Who controls curriculum controls what counts as educated — this is why education is always a political battleground.
