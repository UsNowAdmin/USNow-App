Good

What's right, what works, or what feels nice — used as if they're the same.
How We Say It
good
Where It Comes From
Old English gōd — virtuous, desirable, fitting. Proto-Germanic root. Separate from the word for God — a common misconception. Related to the German gut and Dutch goed.
How It's Been Used
Carries moral, functional, and aesthetic meanings simultaneously — 'a good person,' 'a good tool,' 'a good painting' use the same word for different things. The conflation is rarely noticed but shapes how moral arguments are made: things that work are assumed to be right.
