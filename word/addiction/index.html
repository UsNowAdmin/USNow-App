Addiction

When we feed our body what it wants beyond the point of what it needs — drugs, money, power, or anything.
How We Say It
uh·DIK·shun
Where It Comes From
Latin addictio — a giving over, formal handing over. From addicere — to assign, surrender, bind. In Roman law, the formal delivery of a debtor to a creditor — the debtor became bound to the creditor's service. The medical and psychological meanings developed centuries later.
How It's Been Used
Originally moral and legal — addiction meant being formally bound to something. Shifted to medical use in the late 19th century with the rise of pharmacology and asylum medicine. 'Addict' as a noun for a person dependent on drugs is largely 20th century. Now covers behavioral as well as chemical dependence — gambling, food, screens — though the medical community still debates the boundaries.
