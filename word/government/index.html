Government

Servant to the people or threat to liberty — usually the same institution.
How We Say It
guv·ərn·mənt
Where It Comes From
Latin gubernare — to steer, to pilot a ship. From Greek kybernan — same meaning. The governing metaphor was always navigation — finding a course through unpredictable conditions. Cybernetics and governor share the same root.
How It's Been Used
In American political culture, 'government' became a term of suspicion in the late 20th century. Reagan's 1981 claim that 'government is not the solution; government is the problem' crystallized an anti-government politics that has since shaped policy for decades.
