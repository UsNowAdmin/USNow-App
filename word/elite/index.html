Elite

Those enriched by capitalism and those democracy can't touch.
How We Say It
i·leet
Where It Comes From
French élite — choice, selected. From Old French eslite — chosen. From Latin eligere — to choose, select. Originally a neutral term for the chosen or selected — without the resentment the word now carries.
How It's Been Used
Became a pejorative in populist politics of the 20th and 21st centuries. 'Elite' now implies unearned privilege and disconnection from ordinary life — deployed against academics, media figures, and politicians regardless of actual wealth or power.
