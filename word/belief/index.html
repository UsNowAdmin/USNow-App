Belief

Conviction without proof — faith, trust, or opinion — often used interchangeably.
How We Say It
bi·leef
Where It Comes From
Old English geleafa — trust, confidence. Related to the German Glaube. Rooted in Proto-Germanic words meaning 'to love' or 'to hold dear.' Originally closer to trust in a person than acceptance of a proposition.
How It's Been Used
Flattened over time to mean any held opinion. The phrase 'I believe' now covers everything from theological conviction to preference. The conflation of belief with fact is one of the defining rhetorical moves of contemporary discourse.
