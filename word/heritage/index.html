Heritage

Pride, mythology, or inheritance — rarely examined.
How We Say It
her·i·tij
Where It Comes From
Old French heritage — inheritance, legacy. From heriter — to inherit. From Latin heres — heir. Originally strictly legal — what you were entitled to inherit — before becoming cultural and emotional.
How It's Been Used
'Heritage' carries a soft glow that 'history' doesn't — it implies something worth preserving, something to be proud of. 'Heritage sites,' 'heritage language,' 'Southern heritage' — each use involves a claim about what deserves to be passed forward, and what does not.
